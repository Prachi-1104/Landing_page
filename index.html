Reindeer, also known as caribou in North America, are large, migratory deer with antlers that are shed and regrown annually. They are well-adapted to cold environments and are known for their endurance and strength. Reindeer play a significant role in the culture and mythology of Arctic and Subarctic peoples.

Foxes are small to medium-sized mammals with bushy tails and sharp, intelligent eyes. They are highly adaptable and can thrive in various environments, including forests, mountains, and even urban areas. Known for their cunning nature, foxes are skilled hunters that often hunt alone.

Elephants are the largest land animals on Earth, renowned for their intelligence, strong family bonds, and impressive memory. They have large ears that help regulate body temperature and a trunk that serves as a multipurpose tool for eating, drinking, and social interaction.

The tiger is one of the most majestic and powerful big cats, known for its striking orange coat with black stripes. Tigers are solitary hunters and are at the top of the food chain in their natural habitats. They can be found in diverse environments, from dense jungles to snowy forests.
